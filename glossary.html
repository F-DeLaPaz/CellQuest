üìö BioForge Dispatch Glossary

This glossary explains all key terms, processes, cells, transports, and diagnoses used in BioForge Dispatch missions. Use it anytime to review terminology and biological functions encountered in gameplay.

üî¨ Core Processes & Systems

  • Apoptosis: Programmed cell death that safely eliminates unnecessary or damaged cells after mitosis or incorrect production.
  • BioForge: The cellular production facility where stem cells divide via mitosis to create responder cells for dispatch missions.
  • COO-Key: Chief Operations Organizer ‚Äî your AI mission supervisor, provides feedback, override support, and dispatch guidance.
  • CTS (Cellular Transport System): Delivery network that routes responder cells to correct tissue using lymphatic, blood, or epithelial pathways.
  • iPSCs (Induced Pluripotent Stem Cells): Master stem cells capable of becoming any specialized cell type needed for repair or immune response.
  • Mitosis: Cell division process where one cell splits into two identical daughter cells, producing responders for healing and defense.

üöö Transport Systems

  • Coronary Circulation: Specialized blood vessels that deliver cells directly to heart muscle tissue.
  • Epithelial Migration: Cells move across skin or mucosal surfaces to repair and rebuild tissues (e.g., keratinocytes, goblet cells).
  • Lymphatic Vessels: Pathways that transport immune cells like phagocytes and mast cells to infection or injury sites.
  • Vascular System (Bloodstream): Main circulatory system delivering oxygen, nutrients, and certain responder cells through arteries, veins, and capillaries.

üß¨ Cells

  • Cardiomyocyte: Specialized heart muscle cells responsible for heart contraction and pumping blood through the body.
  • Erythrocyte (Red Blood Cell): Transports oxygen throughout the bloodstream to tissues; not involved in immune defense or repair.
  • Fibroblast: Produces collagen and connective tissue, stabilizing damaged tissues during wound healing and scar formation.
  • Goblet Cell: Secretes mucus to trap pathogens and particles along mucous membranes, protecting airway and eye surfaces.
  • Keratinocyte: Skin barrier cells that regenerate and repair epidermis after damage or infection; rebuild skin structure.
  • Mast Cell: Triggers allergic responses by releasing histamine and other chemicals to recruit immune defense in mucosal tissues.
  • Melanocyte: Produces melanin pigment that protects skin by absorbing ultraviolet (UV) radiation from sunlight.
  • Neuron: Nerve cell that transmits electrical signals for sensation, motor function, and coordination; not involved in healing.
  • Osteocyte: Mature bone cell involved in maintaining bone structure; not relevant for immune or soft tissue repair.
  • Phagocyte: White blood cell that engulfs and digests foreign invaders such as bacteria, fungi, or debris to fight infection.
  • Platelet: Small blood fragments that initiate blood clotting when injury causes bleeding; not involved in cellular repair.

ü©∫ Diagnoses

  • Allergic Conjunctivitis: Eye inflammation triggered by allergens, causing redness, itching, swelling, and watery discharge.
  • Arrhythmia: Abnormal heart rhythm causing irregular, too fast, or too slow heartbeat patterns.
  • Athlete's Foot (Tinea Pedis): Fungal skin infection between toes causing itching, peeling, redness, and sometimes odor.
  • Bacterial Conjunctivitis (Pink Eye): Bacterial infection of the eye that produces redness, discharge, and irritation.
  • Dry Eye Syndrome: Occurs when eyes fail to produce enough tears, leading to dryness, discomfort, and blurry vision.
  • Eczema: Chronic skin condition that causes dry, itchy, red, and irritated skin, often related to allergies or sensitivities.
  • Glaucoma: Eye disease involving increased eye pressure that can damage the optic nerve, leading to vision loss if untreated.
  • Heart Attack (Myocardial Infarction): Occurs when blood flow to part of the heart muscle is blocked, causing tissue damage.
  • Heat Rash: Skin condition caused by blocked sweat glands, leading to tiny blisters, redness, and itchiness in hot environments.
  • Hypertension: High blood pressure that strains the heart and arteries, increasing risk for cardiovascular complications.
  • Ingrown Nail: When the edge of a toenail grows into surrounding skin, leading to pain, swelling, and possible infection.
  • Myocarditis: Inflammation of the heart muscle, often caused by infection or autoimmune response, weakening heart function.
  • Psoriasis: Autoimmune skin condition where cells multiply too rapidly, forming thick, scaly patches of skin.
  • Sunburn: Skin injury caused by overexposure to ultraviolet (UV) radiation, resulting in redness, tenderness, and peeling skin.
