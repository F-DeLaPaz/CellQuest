BioForge Glossary

Reference guide for key terms, cells, and concepts used throughout your BioForge missions.

üß¨ Cell Types

  • Phagocyte: Immune cell that engulfs and destroys pathogens and debris.
  • Keratinocyte: Main skin cell responsible for rebuilding and maintaining the skin barrier.
  • Mast Cell: Immune cell that releases histamine, triggering allergic responses.
  • Goblet Cell: Specialized epithelial cell that secretes mucus for protecting mucosal surfaces.
  • Melanocyte: Pigment-producing skin cell that generates melanin to protect from UV radiation.
  • Cardiomyocyte: Specialized muscle cell responsible for heart contractions and heartbeat rhythm.
  • Fibroblast: Connective tissue cell that builds extracellular matrix and supports tissue repair.
  • Erythrocyte: Red blood cell that carries oxygen throughout the body (not part of defense systems).
  • Neuron: Nerve cell that transmits electrical signals (not directly involved in repair or defense).
  • Platelet: Small blood component that promotes blood clotting during injury.
  • Osteocyte: Mature bone cell responsible for maintaining bone tissue integrity.

üß¨ Biological Processes

  • Mitosis: Cell division process generating identical cells for growth and repair.
  • Apoptosis: Programmed cell death that safely eliminates damaged or unneeded cells.
  • Fibrosis: Excessive connective tissue growth forming scar tissue after injury.

üß¨ BioForge Systems

  • BioForge: The laboratory engine generating patient-specific stem cells via iPSCs to create replacement cells.
  • CTS (Cellular Transport System): Routes generated cells via lymphatic or circulatory systems to target tissues.

üß¨ Key Molecules & Factors

  • Histamine: Chemical released by mast cells that triggers inflammation and allergy symptoms.
  • Melanin: Pigment produced by melanocytes that protects skin from ultraviolet damage.
  • UV Radiation: Ultraviolet light from sun exposure that can damage skin cells leading to sunburn.

üß¨ Conditions (Bonus Diagnoses)

  • Athlete‚Äôs Foot (Tinea Pedis): Fungal skin infection commonly affecting the feet, especially between toes.
  • Allergic Conjunctivitis: Eye inflammation caused by allergic reactions to environmental allergens.
  • Sunburn: Skin damage resulting from excessive UV exposure causing redness, pain, and peeling.
  • Myocarditis: Inflammation of heart muscle tissue, often from infection or immune response, causing heart dysfunction.
